# Acceptable Use Policy

**Splynek v0.30**
**Last updated: 2026-04-17**

Splynek is a general-purpose download manager. We don't moderate
your downloads, and we don't know what they are. But by using
Splynek, you agree not to use it for the activities listed below.

## You will NOT use Splynek to:

### Content that is never acceptable

1. **Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)** — under any
   circumstance, in any jurisdiction. Use here is a criminal
   offence reportable to NCMEC and equivalent bodies worldwide.
2. **Malware, ransomware, stalkerware, spyware, wipers, or any
   other software intended to harm a computer system or its
   user** — distributing, trafficking, or staging.
3. **Non-consensual intimate imagery** ("revenge porn"), or
   deepfake sexual imagery of any identifiable person.

### Content you don't have the right to

4. **Copyrighted works you don't own or have permission to
   download / share.** Splynek's fleet feature in particular
   means a file you set to "sharable" can be served to other
   LAN peers — that distribution is your responsibility.
5. **Trade secrets, proprietary source code, classified
   information, or other material you are contractually or
   legally prohibited from acquiring.**

### Infrastructure abuse

6. **Denial-of-service.** Splynek aggressively opens parallel
   connections. Using it to saturate someone else's server is
   abuse. Respect `robots.txt`, rate limits, and terms of service
   of the origins you're downloading from.
7. **Circumventing access controls.** If a site requires
   authentication, subscription, paywall, geographic restriction,
   or CAPTCHA, don't use Splynek to work around it unless you
   have an explicit right to do so (e.g. your own account,
   legitimate interoperability under EU Directive 2009/24/EC,
   archival / accessibility exceptions in your jurisdiction).
8. **Port-scanning / recon / reconnaissance.** Splynek's fleet
   listener is not a tool for probing strangers' networks.

### Privacy violations

9. **Harvesting personal data** — mailing lists, leaked
   databases, scraped PII, doxing targets. Splynek will
   happily download a multi-gigabyte file for you; we're not
   interested in helping you move people's private data.
10. **Stalking, harassment, or targeted abuse.** If Splynek is
    part of a pattern of harassment against a real person —
    monitoring their social profiles, archiving their content
    non-consensually, tracking their public online presence at
    scale — stop using Splynek.

### Network effect abuse

11. **Using the fleet feature to silently redistribute
    content you wouldn't share in person.** The LAN content
    cache is designed for a household or small team pooling
    legitimate resources. It is not a piracy network by
    default, and you undertake not to turn it into one.

## What happens if you violate this policy

Two things. One: your licence to use Splynek terminates
automatically (see EULA §9). Two: the maintainers reserve the
right to cooperate with law-enforcement investigations to the
extent required by law. Splynek doesn't log your activity, so
there isn't much we could share even if we wanted to — but if
we ever become aware of a user engaging in the conduct above,
we can and will decline to provide them support, refunds, or
updates, and will note their account (if one exists) as
suspended.

## What if you're not sure?

Ask. `info@splynek.app` or open an issue on the repo. We'd
rather answer a question than miss a problem.
